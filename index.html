    How Julia S. Choe, Assistant United States Attorney, Spends Her Sundays

    Sunday Routine

    By Jonathan Deng NOV. 19, 2017

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Julia Sun Choe, 30, currently an Assistant United States attorney in the criminal division of the Central District of California, lives on the west side of Los Angeles with her boyfriend Jon (“he wakes up a lot earlier than me on weekends and putzs around doing God knows what”), 28, and dog Tonsai, 5, a Labrador-Corgi mix. Prior to working as a federal prosecutor, Choe worked at various law firms in Los Angeles and San Francisco and was a dancer on the Harvard Crimson Dance Team.

    Continue reading the main story

    BREAK OF DAWN (not really) I like to sleep in on weekends because it’s the only day that I don’t have to get to work and fight LA traffic. It’s so warm in bed, and I have a down comforter, so in the mornings I just like to turn myself into a burrito and snuggle. If [our dog] Tonsai sees me stir, he’ll jump onto the bed and try to fight for my spot. He’s really kind of entitled.

    Continue reading the main story

    A BRISK WALK AND BREAKFAST After waking up around 10am, Tonsai will need to do his business so we take him to Virginia Avenue Park, which is also conveniently across the street. They have this great farmer’s market there on Saturday mornings, which I love. I usually get tomatoes, oyster mushrooms, and peaches in the summer. Walking Tonsai around the farmer’s market and filling my bag is one of the highlights of the week. I don’t really cook so when we get home, Jon is in charge of breakfast while I follow him around as his sous chef. The best thing I’ve learned is how to cut a ripe tomato and eat it on a white bread sandwich with a little bit of mayo and salt.

    Continue reading the main story

    SHOP BOP After breakfast, I try to do something chill and not think about the upcoming work week. I recently bought a new purse so I don’t need anything, but it’s fun to check out different areas in L.A. like the Hayden Tract. Because I live in Santa Monica and work downtown, I rarely get to visit the areas in between. I tried Reformation [a store that allows shoppers to choose their styles on touchscreens and deliver the appropriate styles to their dressing room], but I couldn’t really get into the experience. The zipper on my dress broke in the dressing room and the girl working at the store had to come help me get out of it. That’s where the whole technology thing broke down.

    Continue reading the main story

    WORK ON THE WEEKEND Because my case load [as a federal prosecutor] is so heavy, I usually have to do some work on the weekends. I’ve tried logging in remotely from home, but our IT is so slow that it’s often faster to just drive downtown and work from the office. Plus I concentrate better and get a lot done when the office is quiet and I don’t have to wear a suit on the weekends. One of the cases I worked on is getting appealed to the 9th Circuit (the federal appeals court that reviews cases tried in California), so I want to draft my brief for that. Working as a prosecutor is my dream job, and I’m getting a lot of trial experience, but it is insanely difficult, and sometimes I don’t feel up to it. But I work really hard and things always work out in the end.

    Continue reading the main story

    ASIAN FAMILY DINNER Jon has these friends that he goes rock climbing with, and I invite some law school friends that settled in LA—we have this tradition where we try to meet up at a different Asian restaurant every Sunday night. Although last weekend, we went to Cassia for pizza and pasta, so it’s not really a hard rule. Our favorite ramen place in LA, Tsujita, is really close to us, but usually there is an hour wait, so we go someplace more chill like Slurpin’ Ramen or Kitchen Story, or this cool Indonesian restaurant/ grocery store: Simpang Asia. It’s nice to get together and complain about our jobs. I feel like people our age are working a lot, but not settled in our careers and relationships yet, and it causes a lot of stress. It’s nice to take some time out and remember the important things.

    Continue reading the main story

    THE CROWN After dinner, I usually like to watch Netflix before going to sleep of the night. I just finished the first season of the Crown recently. I like that the show features a strong woman protagonist, but I hate how she fails to keep her promises to family members, especially about their freedom to marry who they want. I get into bed around midnight and watch dance videos. I like the BYU Cougarettes, whom I once competed against at Dance Team Nationals, and I always check in to see how my old team, the Crimson Dance Team, is doing.
